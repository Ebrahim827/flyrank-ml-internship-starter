# Research Question Framing: Content Refresh Prioritization

## 1. Approved Lane Choice
* **Selected Lane:** Content Refresh Queue

## 2. Core Question & Unit of Analysis
* **The Question:** Which high-traffic pages are showing early signs of traffic decline and require immediate content updates to protect company revenue?
* **Unit of Analysis:** A single URL (unique page) belonging to a client website.

## 3. The Output & Action
* **The Output:** A prioritized "Refresh Queue" list of the top 50 URLs most likely to be in a downward trend, sorted by their traffic exposure risk.
* **The Action:** An SEO manager or content writer will take this top 50 list and manually update, expand, or rewrite the content on those specific pages.

## 4. The Cost of a Wrong Recommendation
* **False Positive (Flagging a healthy page):** The company wastes valuable writer time and money rewriting a page that was completely fine, hence disrupting its current stablity.
* **False Negative (Missing a dying page):** A high-traffic page continues to crash silently in the background, causing the company to lose search visibility, clicks, and revenue to competitors.

## 5. Why Data/ML Helps (Instead of Just "Training a Model")
This is not just an exercise in running an ML algorithm. A simple rule like "look at old pages" fails because data is messy—some old pages stay stable forever, while fresh pages can crash due to algorithmic shifts. Machine learning is necessary because it can look at complex, simultaneous interactions between clicks, positions, and history across thousands of pages to spot true anomalies that a human looking at a spreadsheet would completely miss.
